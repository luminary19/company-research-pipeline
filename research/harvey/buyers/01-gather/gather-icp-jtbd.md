# Gather: Buyers — ICP + Jobs-to-be-Done + Buyer Journey

## Scope

Covered: (1) ICP segments and who actually buys, with penetration where sourced; (2) buyer personas and the decision unit, incl. named change agents; (3) jobs-to-be-done by practice area and where humans stay; (4) the buyer journey / GTM motion (champion pilot -> forward-deployed deployment -> firmwide, procurement, security/privilege review, Lighthouse/design-partner co-development, land-and-expand); (5) named case studies with disclosed ROI.

Excluded (sibling slices own): total market sizing / TAM math; pricing mechanics; the buyer-side skeptic / adoption-friction case; per-competitor comparison. Funding/traction, product surfaces, and pricing are pre-established context and are only referenced where load-bearing for the demand-side question.

## Findings

### 2. Buyer personas & the decision unit

- The **decision unit is a "federation of partner-owned practices"**: a firmwide "land" is meaningless if individual practice-group partners veto adoption; "even the CIO cannot force a senior equity partner to adopt anything." Deployment touches **three distinct populations with incompatible adoption triggers — partners, associates, and knowledge-management lawyers.** [R3·C2] (Perspective AI, 2026-05-18, <https://getperspective.ai/blog/harvey-ai-forward-deployed-engineers-biglaw-deployment-playbook-2026>)

### 3. Jobs-to-be-done by practice area

- Harvey's own JTBD framing per solution track: **Transactional** = "accelerate due diligence, contract analysis, and review"; **Litigation** = "reduce manual effort, prioritize strategy"; **In-House** = "streamline work and shift focus to strategy and speed." [R1·C3] (Harvey, Customers, <https://www.harvey.ai/customers>)
- Independent ranking of most-deployed workflows: **(1) Contract review and analysis** — "the most-deployed workflow because it has the cleanest before/after measurement" (partner reviews a contract in 25 min instead of 90); **(2) Due diligence** — document classification, cross-document pattern recognition, red-flag surfacing, report generation in M&A; **(3) Legal research** — querying legal/regulatory/tax sources with a fine-tuned model (the Harvey + LexisNexis 2025 partnership brought primary law content in). [R3·C2] (Ascero AI, <https://asceroai.com/news/harvey-big-law-ai-2026-deployment-data>)
- A&O's practitioner framing groups the work into three jobs: **(1) Legal research** (improving accuracy, fewer false positives/negatives), **(2) Information extraction** (summarizing/reorganizing), **(3) Document creation** (drafting from emails to contract provisions). [R3·C3, firm-disclosed via session] (Platforum9, 2024-11-08, <https://platforum9.com/lessons-from-the-ai-frontline-how-ao-sherman-implemented-harvey/>)
- Concrete task list from an in-house deployment (Deutsche Telekom): identifying critical sections in lengthy contracts (a 200-page document in one example), condensing complex regulations into summaries, specialized legal research, and accelerating tender-response generation; use across Legal, Compliance, and Data Protection. [R3·C3, firm-disclosed] (Applied, 2026-04-11, <https://theapplied.co/use-cases/how-deutsche-telekom-saves-5-hours-per-week-per-attorney-with-harvey>)
- Practice-area breadth from firm press: Stinson attorneys used Harvey for automated transcript review, deposition-outline generation, standard clause analysis, M&A due-diligence summarization, litigation trend analysis, and clause drafting. Bayer cites IP/patent drafting and redlining (one team member reported avoiding outside-counsel costs), R&D/clinical-trial/M&A/procurement contracts, and compliance summaries. [R1·C1] (Stinson, <https://www.stinson.com/newsroom-news-stinson-adopts-harvey-firmwide-to-enhance-client-service-and-collaboration>; Harvey/Bayer, <https://www.harvey.ai/customers/bayer>)
- **Tax as the explicitly-targeted next vertical**: PwC alliance builds domain-specific models for tax, legal, and deals; independent analysis notes "Harvey's post-Series E roadmap explicitly targets tax advisory as the next vertical, and PwC's tax practice is the proof point." [R1·C1 / R3·C3] (Harvey/PwC UK, <https://www.harvey.ai/customers/pwc-uk>; Ascero AI, <https://asceroai.com/news/harvey-big-law-ai-2026-deployment-data>)
- **Where humans stay (JTBD boundary)**: independent analysis states Harvey is "concentrated in high-volume document-heavy work" and that **"advocacy, oral argument preparation, novel-issue legal strategy, or client counsel... remain partner-only tasks at every firm in the disclosed customer list."** Firms "kept those tasks human and pushed the gains into the high-volume document-heavy workflows." PwC's own posture: "AI will not be used to provide legal advice to clients and AI will not replace lawyers" ("human led and technology enabled"). [R3·C2 / R2·C2] (Ascero AI, <https://asceroai.com/news/harvey-big-law-ai-2026-deployment-data>; Legal Dive, <https://www.legaldive.com/news/pwc-generative-ai-harvey-openai-legal-work-large-language-models/648569/>)

### 4. The buyer journey / GTM motion

- **Lighthouse program (design-partner co-development)**: an initiative where "Harvey partnered with a group of law firms to co-develop the platform," embedding the tech inside organizations like Allen & Overy and PwC to test on complex workflows (per co-founder Gabriel Pereyra). Australia is being used to "co-build" via a "listening tour" and working sessions with innovation leaders at Ashurst and Clayton Utz. [R2·C3, founder-sourced] (Forbes Australia, <https://www.forbes.com.au/news/innovation/what-is-harvey-ai-the-legal-tech-decacorn-changing-how-lawyers-work/>)
- **Forward-deployed-engineer (FDE) deployment model**: a full firm rollout takes **6-9 months** and the embedded FDE runs three overlapping phases — Firm-discovery (weeks 1-6, 40-80 hrs of qualitative interviews), Workflow-mapping (weeks 4-14; picks ~2-3 concrete workflows, one transactional + one advisory), Knowledge-base-build (weeks 8-28; ingests the firm's precedent vault into RAG indexes respecting matter walls). Integration targets iManage, NetDocuments, Aderant, Intapp; "there is no launch day," just partner-by-partner conversion. FDE function estimated at 40-80 engineers as of early 2026. [R3·C2] (Perspective AI, <https://getperspective.ai/blog/harvey-ai-forward-deployed-engineers-biglaw-deployment-playbook-2026>)
- **Six-stage enterprise sales cycle** (independent reverse-engineering): (1) Awareness/credibility via warm referral + Legal Engineer first call (weeks 1-4, no product shown); (2) **Hyper-personalized demo** rebuilt around the prospect's actual cases/contracts/deals, prospect invited to "fight with the model" (weeks 4-8); (3) **Pilot** — multi-week, real lawyers/real matters, Legal Engineer + Product Specialist embedded, usage data collected to build the ROI case (weeks 8-16+); (4) **Security & compliance review, run in parallel** — SOC 2, ISO 27001, DPA, zero-training commitment pre-answered to "remove the normal 3-6 month security review bottleneck"; (5) Commercial negotiation (weeks 12-20, 12-month minimums); (6) **Onboarding & expansion** (months 6-18) with Workflow Builder co-creating proprietary workflows. Overall cycle 3-6 months. [R3·C3] (Sev Austinov, <https://sevaustinov.me/hypergrowth-research/companies/harvey.html>)
- **Champion-led pilot -> firmwide is the documented pattern.** A&O: sandbox with a limited "ring-fenced" group, identify use cases/risks + governance, then gradual firmwide expansion, led by the Markets Innovation Group (lawyers + software engineers + technologists). CMS: **structured 12-month pilot of 300 lawyers** on Assistant/Vault/Knowledge/Workflow Agents, scaling to 7,200 firmwide across 50+ countries. Ashurst: a five-month experiment across several GenAI platforms and 23 offices / 525+ pilot users before global rollout. Stinson: 100+ attorney pilot. Faegre Drinker: "rigorous, multi-phase evaluation." [R1·C1 / R3·C2] (Harvey/CMS, <https://www.harvey.ai/blog/cms-expands-use-of-harvey-firmwide-across-50-countries>; Applied/CMS, <https://theapplied.co/use-cases/how-cms-uses-harvey-to-scale-ai-to-7200-lawyers-globally>; Ashurst/Global Legal Post, <https://www.globallegalpost.com/news/ashurst-agrees-global-partnership-with-legal-ai-platform-harvey-473224571>; MMNTM, <https://www.mmntm.net/articles/harvey-deep-dive>)
- **Security / privilege review is the gating step**, not an afterthought. Per the ABA 2024 Legal Technology Survey (cited by Perspective AI), **79% of firms with 100+ attorneys cite client confidentiality as the #1 barrier to AI adoption** — ahead of cost or accuracy. Harvey's answer: tenant-level isolation, matter-level access controls, ethical-wall integration, audit logging, zero-data-training commitment, and (per firm-disclosed cases) data residency guarantees (Deutsche Telekom: data stored in Germany). [R3·C2 / R1·C2] (Perspective AI, <https://getperspective.ai/blog/harvey-ai-forward-deployed-engineers-biglaw-deployment-playbook-2026>; Harvey/Deutsche Telekom, <https://www.harvey.ai/customers/deutsche-telekom>)
- **Harvey's official "Transformation Office"** productizes change management: it deploys legal engineering, customer success, and legal innovation teams to align on metrics, phases of adoption, milestones, and outcomes; "repeatable, structured playbooks" covering Technology, Skills, Communications, Structures, Governance; explicitly aims to "create champions throughout the business." [R1·C3, self-described] (Harvey blog, <https://www.harvey.ai/blog/introducing-harveys-transformation-office>)
- **Land-and-expand economics**: "Start with a few hundred seats in research, drafting, and due diligence. Median seat count doubles within 12 months," with expansion proposals targeting underutilized practice groups and custom workflows creating switching costs (25,000+ custom Vault workflow agents cited; first 50 enterprise customers were all referrals). [R3·C3] (Sev Austinov, <https://sevaustinov.me/hypergrowth-research/companies/harvey.html>)
- **FOMO / reference selling as the demand engine**: after A&O's announcement, "every competing firm's managing partner called their innovation team the same morning"; "Harvey sold fear of being left behind." A&O and PwC were turned into a de-facto reseller network ("Harvey, powered by PwC"; A&O sells Harvey-built agentic workflows to other firms/clients). [R3·C3 / R2·C2] (Su Belagodu / Substack, 2026-04-05, <https://subelagodu.substack.com/p/week-20-the-harvey-playbook-journey>; Above the Law, <https://abovethelaw.com/2025/06/what-harvey-got-that-the-rest-of-us-missed/>)
- **Emerging commercial model beyond seats**: "selling the work" — revenue-share arrangements where Harvey co-builds workflows and splits revenue when firms sell AI-enhanced flat-fee services to clients; PwC's Deals team is encoding core activities into "modular workflows and agents" (Agent Builder) as "reusable, client-ready assets." [R3·C3 / R1·C1] (Sev Austinov, <https://sevaustinov.me/hypergrowth-research/companies/harvey.html>; Harvey/PwC deals, <https://www.harvey.ai/blog/pwc-shift-toward-ai-enabled-deal-execution>)
