## 2. Current posture (mid-2026) — a three-pronged land-grab

> Purpose: the strategy the timeline reveals, read as intent.

**2.1 Own the model layer — cheaply.** Having conceded the model race (its 2024 model was beaten by frontier), Harvey pivoted to **post-training open weights (GLM-5.1) for cost + control**, plus an open-source legal-foundation-model series (targeted ~Dec 2026), aiming to "serve frontier intelligence affordably" [I/80%]. Read: a defensive-and-offensive move to escape frontier-lab cost dependence and to give firms "their own intelligence."

**2.2 Own the agent + ecosystem layer.** Agent Builder, 25k+ custom agents, Connector Library (MCP), Datasite/DeepJudge/iManage integrations, and the Transformation Office point at a strategy of **becoming the connective tissue of the legal stack** — the "operating system for legal" thesis, executed via land-and-expand [I/78%]. Many launches are Early Access (design-partner-led), consistent with a services-heavy GTM.

**2.3 Expand the market.** Corporate in-house revenue 4%→41% in ~12 months; international offices (Singapore, Sydney, Bengaluru, Toronto, Milan); benchmark internationalization (BLB: Global). The expansion narrative (tax/finance/compliance/professional services) is what justifies the valuation [I/72%].

**2.4 The pressure the posture is responding to.** All of this is happening *as the foundation labs enter legal directly* (Anthropic connectors, OpenAI's Boehmig hire) — so the posture reads as **racing to entrench distribution + data + services moats before the labs commoditize the layer** [I/75%] (thesis §7 A6; competitive §5).
